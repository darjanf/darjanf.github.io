Goals of Scott Bessent’s 3-3-3 Plan

Scott Bessent’s 3-3-3 Plan, inspired by Japan’s “Three Arrows” strategy, aims to transform the U.S. economy by 2028 through three key objectives:

  • 3% Real GDP Growth: Achieve sustained 3% annual real GDP growth via deregulation, tax reforms, and AI-driven productivity gains.
  • 3% Budget Deficit: Reduce the federal deficit to 3% of GDP from 6–7%, requiring significant spending cuts or revenue increases.
  • 3 Million Barrels/Day Oil Production: Increase U.S. energy production by the equivalent of 3 million barrels of oil per day to lower energy costs and inflation.

These goals target debt stabilization, economic growth, and energy security, but face challenges from demographics, fiscal constraints, and market dynamics.

Real GDP Year-Over-Year (YoY)

Conclusion

US Real GDP YoY growth was stable at 2.5% through Q3 2024, peaked at 2.8% in Q4 2024, but slowed to 2.0% in Q1 2025, potentially signaling cautious investor sentiment and increased market volatility.

[](https://www.bea.gov/)

Real GDP Quarter-over-Quarter (QoQ, Annualized)

Conclusion

QoQ GDP growth peaked at 3.0% in Q2 2024 but turned negative (-0.3%) in Q1 2025, indicating potential economic contraction that could pressure financial markets and spur risk-averse trading.

[](https://www.bea.gov/)

Annual Real GDP Growth

Conclusion

Annual GDP growth fell from 5.8% in 2021 to 1.94% in 2022 and rose to 2.9% in 2023, reflecting uneven recovery, which may temper financial market optimism for sustained high growth.

Interim Conclusion: GDP Goal Assessment

Scott Bessent’s 3-3-3 Plan targets 3% real GDP growth by 2028. Current data shows YoY growth at 2.0% (Q1 2025), annual growth at 2.9% (2023), and QoQ growth at -0.3% (Q1 2025), all below the 3% target. The economy faces challenges from an aging population, slowing labor force growth, and potential fiscal cuts, with CBO projecting long-term growth at 1.9%.

[](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/scott-bessents-3-3-3-plan-will-turn-out-more-like-2-6-0-213848777.html)[](https://www.bea.gov/)

Prognosis: Achieving 3% growth by 2028 is possible if deregulation, AI-driven productivity, and tax reforms are effectively implemented, but demographic and fiscal constraints make it unlikely before 2027, assuming steady policy efforts and no major economic shocks.
